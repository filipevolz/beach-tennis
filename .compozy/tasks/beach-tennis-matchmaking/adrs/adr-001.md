# ADR-001: Balanced Marketplace MVP Centered on Open Match Participation

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-05-16

## Context

The product must serve two sides from the start: arenas and clubs that need to fill open time slots, and players who struggle to find compatible partners and opponents for beach tennis matches. The user prioritized a balanced outcome in the first three months, measured by growth in both active players and active venues. The MVP must support both doubles and singles while preserving a strong experience for both sides.

## Decision

Build the MVP as a balanced marketplace. Arenas and clubs will publish available courts and times, while players will discover open matches, join available spots, and look for partners or opponents based on location and playing level. The product will prioritize open match participation as the core interaction that connects venue supply with player demand.

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: Arena-First With Simple Player Discovery

- **Description**: Prioritize venue scheduling and occupancy, while offering only basic player-side discovery of available games.
- **Pros**: Simpler commercial story for venues, easier to focus early operations on supply.
- **Cons**: Weaker player experience, lower chance of solving the main user pain of not finding people to play with.
- **Why rejected**: It under-serves the player need that makes demand generation possible and weakens marketplace balance.

### Alternative 2: Player-First With Venues as a Supporting Catalog

- **Description**: Prioritize matchmaking between players and treat venues mainly as a list of places to play.
- **Pros**: Strong focus on the social matching problem, potentially faster early player engagement.
- **Cons**: Weak value proposition for arenas and clubs, less control over reliable availability and booking behavior.
- **Why rejected**: It does not meet the requirement to create a strong experience for venues and players at the same time.

## Consequences

### Positive

- Aligns the MVP with the two-sided business goal from day one.
- Creates a clearer loop between venue inventory and player participation.
- Supports the main user pain: players can find people and places in one journey.

### Negative

- MVP scope must stay disciplined to avoid becoming too broad.
- Early operations may require parallel activation of both venues and players.

### Risks

- Marketplace cold-start risk if one side grows faster than the other.
- Risk of low-quality matches if player level and match expectations are unclear.
- Risk of friction for venues if publishing availability is too manual.

## Implementation Notes

This ADR defines product scope and prioritization only. Technical design belongs in the TechSpec stage.
